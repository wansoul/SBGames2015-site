Computação

The goal of the SBGames 2015 - Computing Track is to advance knowledge related to Computing for Games and Digital Entertainment, by bringing together researchers and fostering discussion about the state of the art in the field.

Paper Submission

We invite contributions on all aspects of Computing for Games and Digital Entertainment. Submissions can be FULL PAPERS about results from novel research (written in ENGLISH, up to 10 pages long) or SHORT PAPERS describing work in progress or presenting less-mature results (written in PORTUGUESE or ENGLISH, up to 4 pages long).

All contributions must be original, unpublished work. Any work that has previously been published or has been simultaneously submitted in a similar form to any other conference or journal will be rejected. Accepted papers will be published in the Conference Proceedings. Please note that at least one author of an accepted paper MUST register for and attend the symposium AND present the work for it to be published in the proceedings.

Users of LateX and Microsoft Word are required to use, respectively, the following templates for preparation of their submissions: Latex Template or Word Template.

Papers should be submitted electronically through JEMS system (http://submissoes.sbc.org.br). Submissions will be subjected to a double blind review, which means that both the reviewer and author identities are concealed from the reviewers, and vice versa, throughout the review process. Thus, authors should avoid including information that allow their identification (names, affiliations, explicit self-references, project names, acknowledgment, etc). Detailed instructions including paper formatting will be available soon at the conference web site. The authors of the best papers may be invited to submit an extended version of their papers to be published in a specialized journal.
